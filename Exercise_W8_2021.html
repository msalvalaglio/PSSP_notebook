Week 8 - Exercise¶

Problem Statement¶

An aqueous feed of 10000 kg h\(^-1\), saturated with \(BaCl_2\) at 100\(^o\)C, enters a crystallizer that can be simulated with the MSMPR model. The slurry leaves the crystallizer at 20\(^o\)C with crystals of the dihydrate. The crystallizer has a volume of 2.0 \(m^3\). From laboratory experiments, the crystal growth rate is essentially constant at \(4.0\times{10^{-7}}\) m/s. The density of the dihydrate crystals is 3.097 g cm\(^{-3}\). The density of an aqueous, saturated solution of barium chloride at 20\(^o\)C is 1.29 g cm\(^{-3}\). The solubility of \(BaCl_2\) is 58.3 g/100 g water at 100\(^o\) C and 35.7 g/100 g water at 20\(^o\) C. The molar mass of \(BaCl_2\) is 208.27 g/mol, the the dihydrate, \(BaCl_2\dot2H_2O\) has a molar mass of 244.31 g/mol. The density of the crystals is 3097 \(kg/m^3\), the density of the solution in the crystallizer is 1279 \(kg/m^3\)
